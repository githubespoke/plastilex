About & Methodology

PlastiLex project overview, legal constraints, taxonomy construction, and gap scoring methodology.

Project Overview

PlastiLex is a research gap analysis platform focused on Chinese-language academic literature on microplastics contamination. China is the world's largest producer and user of plastics, and its research community has generated a substantial body of scientific work — much of which remains inaccessible to English-speaking researchers due to language barriers and limited translation coverage.

This dashboard surfaces where the gap is largest, so translators, editors, and platform operators can prioritize resources toward the highest-impact sub-topics.

Legal & Licensing Constraints

PlastiLex operates under a strict open-access mandate. All source materials must be either:

  • Published under a Creative Commons (CC-BY or CC-BY-SA) license, or
  • In the public domain, or
  • Accompanied by explicit written permission from the original publisher or author.

No CNKI-sourced papers may be included in the public deliverable without publisher authorisation. Papers sourced via ResearchGate, Sci-Hub, or informal sharing channels are excluded. Where permission has been requested and is pending, papers are staged but embargoed from the public corpus.

Metadata (titles, author affiliations, journal names, DOIs) is treated as factual and is excluded from copyright protection in most jurisdictions; only the full-text translations trigger licensing considerations.

Taxonomy Construction

The eight taxonomy tags were derived by:

  • Reviewing 400+ abstract-level records from CNKI and CQVIP (2015–2024) using keyword clustering
  • Cross-referencing against the Web of Science Microplastics subject category hierarchy
  • Consolidating into mutually-exclusive, collectively-exhaustive buckets that cover >95% of the corpus
  • Pilot-testing inter-rater reliability with two independent Chinese-reading researchers (κ = 0.83)

Each paper is assigned exactly one primary tag. Future iterations may support multi-tagging.

Gap Score Calculation

The Gap Score is defined as:

Gap Score = (# Chinese-language papers in tag) ÷ (# English-language papers on same topic globally)

A score of 1.0 indicates parity. Scores above 1.0 mean more Chinese research exists than English coverage — higher values indicate larger translation gaps. Scores below 1.0 indicate the English literature already exceeds the Chinese base.

English paper counts are sourced from Web of Science and Scopus full-text searches using equivalent keyword sets. Chinese counts are sourced from CNKI categorical searches with title/abstract verification.

This is a relative metric and should be read alongside absolute counts: a tag with 3 Chinese and 1 English paper yields a score of 3.0, but has far lower absolute priority than a tag with 120 Chinese and 40 English papers (score 3.0, but 160 papers of total research activity).

Data Sources

  • Chinese corpus: CNKI (中国知网), CQVIP (维普期刊), Wanfang Data (万方数据)
  • English comparison base: Web of Science Core Collection, Scopus
  • DOI resolution: CrossRef REST API
  • License verification: Unpaywall API + manual publisher lookup
